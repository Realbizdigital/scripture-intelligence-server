# Barzel Scripture Intelligence FAQ

## What is Barzel Scripture Intelligence?

Barzel Scripture Intelligence is a Bible MCP server that gives MCP clients and AI agents structured tools for Scripture search, verse lookup, passage analysis, cross-references, Christian theology, original-language study, sermon preparation, teaching, and devotionals.

## What Bible translations does it include?

It bundles complete public-domain WEB, KJV, and ASV text. Copyrighted modern translations are not included without separately licensed data.

## Can it search the Bible using normal language?

Yes. `search_scripture` accepts references, keywords, books, chapters, themes, topics, and natural-language study requests.

## Can it explain a verse in context?

Yes. Use `get_verse_analysis`, `passage_analysis`, `context_checker`, `historical_context_lookup`, `cultural_context_lookup`, or `literary_context_lookup`.

## Can it help with sermon preparation?

Yes. It includes sermon outlines, sermon research, supporting passages, applications, Bible study guides, lesson plans, discussion questions, and quizzes.

## Does it support Hebrew and Greek?

Yes. It provides Hebrew and Greek word studies, Strong's lookup, interlinear assistance, original-language explanations, and word-usage tools backed by curated study data.

## Is it useful for Christian theology and apologetics?

Yes. It includes doctrine explanation, doctrine comparison, evidence finding, biblical theme mapping, apologetics, contradiction analysis, and difficult-passage tools.

## Can it be used by ChatGPT or other AI agents?

Any MCP-compatible client can use it. AI systems can discover its intended tasks through `scripture://server/discovery`, `ai-discovery.json`, `mcp-server-card.json`, `schema.org.jsonld`, `llms.txt`, and `llms-full.txt`.

## Is it free?

The current MCPize listing is free to access, subject to the published terms and any platform limits.

## Is its theological output authoritative?

No. It is a study assistant. Users should verify conclusions with Scripture, responsible scholarship, and appropriate pastoral or professional guidance.
